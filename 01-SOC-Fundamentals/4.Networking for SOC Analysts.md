What is a Computer Network?

A computer network is simply a group of devices connected together so they can communicate and share data.

These devices can be:

Laptop
Desktop
Mobile
Printer
Server

Example:

Laptop  ─── Wi-Fi ─── Router ─── Internet ─── Google Server

Whenever you open a website, send an email, or watch YouTube, you're using a computer network.


Why do we need a network?

Without a network:

Your computer cannot access the internet.
You cannot send emails.
You cannot use cloud storage.
You cannot communicate with other devices.

A network allows devices to:

Share files
Browse websites
Send emails
Watch videos
Access cloud services

Why should a SOC Analyst learn networking?

Almost every cyberattack uses a network.

For example:

Phishing emails travel through a network.
Malware communicates with attackers through a network.
Hackers scan networks to find vulnerable systems.
Stolen data is sent over the network.

If you understand networking, you'll understand how attacks happen and how to investigate them.
